The Evidence Grade System

Every report carries a grade declaring how far its claims were verified. We would rather publish a B than dress it up as an A.

A
Primary-Verified
Official disclosures, filings, or statistics checked against the original publisher.
B
Headline-Corroborated
Claims verified to headline level across multiple outlets. Article bodies not independently confirmed.
C
Hypothesis
Interpretive signals and projections. Explicitly labeled; never stated as fact.
Fact / Hypothesis Separation
Statements are written only to their verified level; interpretation is always marked as hypothesis.
No Unverified Claims
No performance assertions, no revenue promises, no uncited figures.
Full Source Disclosure
Every report lists its sources and the verification level of each.
7-Day Observation
Published reports are tracked for seven days; results feed the next topic selection.
