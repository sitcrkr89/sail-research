Tracking the HBM Transition: Who Actually Leads the Race to HBM4E?

Jul 08, 2026Sail ResearchSK hynix · Samsung · Micron, 11-record datasetFact / hypothesis / model-prior separated

Ask "who's winning the HBM4E race" and most answers you'll get are a single confident sentence pointing at one company. That's the wrong shape for the question. The honest answer is a matrix — company × generation × evidence quality — and this report is Sail Research's first published run of that matrix, built to show the method as much as the result.

The Model, Not Just the Verdict

We built a company-level scenario score from an 11-record, 30-field dataset tracking SK hynix, Samsung Electronics/Samsung Memory, and Micron across HBM3, HBM3E, HBM4, and HBM4E. Every field in every record carries its own evidence label — Direct Evidence, Requires Verification, or Reasoned Inference — rather than one blanket grade for the whole claim. The scenario score is a transparent rule-based prior, not a trained model and not a probability: high stack height raises transition risk unless offset by thermal, bonding, or customer evidence; Direct Evidence raises confidence, Requires Verification and hypothesis-status records pull it back down.

Where Each Company's Own Data Disagrees With the Verdict

The scenario tags (P10/aggressive, P50/base, P90/delayed-or-watchlist) are more informative than the ranking table alone. Samsung's own top forward record — HBM4E 12H/48GB — sits at P10/aggressive with readiness 0.749, but its other HBM4E record, the 16H/64GB planned lineup, sits at P90/delayed-or-watchlist with readiness dropping to 0.437. Same company, same generation, opposite scenario tag — because one record has product/sample evidence and the other is still a planned-lineup disclosure with production and performance data marked Requires Verification.

SK hynix shows the same internal split: its HBM4 12-layer sample record scores P50/base (readiness 0.626), while its 16-layer HBM4E-class derivative is explicitly labeled Reasoned Inference / Requires Verification and scores P90/delayed-or-watchlist (readiness 0.313, risk 0.79) — the largest single risk score in the dataset. That record exists in the model because of packaging-history extrapolation, not because a company disclosure named it.

What the Model Cannot Yet Tell You

This is an NN-ready schema, not an NN-trained one: the dataset's own documentation flags that future-outcome target labels (next-gen-within-24-months, customer-ramp-success, HBM4E-16H-transition) are currently UNLABELED. The scenario scores are a seed for that future model, not a finished forecast. Treat every number above as an analytic prior built from currently captured public disclosures — not a complete picture of any company's internal roadmap, and not a market-share or revenue prediction.

Primary-Source Verification (July 2026)

The factual anchors under the model are not left at headline level. On 8 July 2026 we captured each company's own official disclosures directly from source and stored them with retrieval date, byte size, and SHA-256 hash. Samsung's own newsroom states it ships industry-first commercial HBM4 and has begun shipment of industry-first HBM4E samples; Micron's investor release documents high-volume HBM4 production designed for NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform; SK hynix's newsroom shows its HBM4 at the TSMC 2025 Technology Symposium and its MR-MUF / hybrid-bonding packaging position. These are Direct Evidence for the presence and stage of each company's HBM4/HBM4E program — the specific URLs are listed in Sources below.

What primary capture does not promote: named-customer yield percentages, qualification timing, and volume-ramp figures remain Requires Verification — the official pages assert shipment and production but do not disclose the granular yield/qualification numbers, so the model does not treat them as fact. The scenario scores themselves stay exactly what they were: transparent priors, not probabilities.

Evidence Caveat — Fact vs. Model Prior

  • Fact (Direct Evidence, primary-sourced) — Each company's HBM4/HBM4E program presence and stage, verified 8 July 2026 against the companies' own official pages and stored with retrieval date + SHA-256: Samsung shipping commercial HBM4 and HBM4E samples; Micron high-volume HBM4 for NVIDIA Vera Rubin; SK hynix HBM4 (TSMC 2025) and MR-MUF/hybrid-bonding packaging. URLs in Sources.
  • Model Prior (transparent rule-based scoring, not a fact) — The overall/readiness/risk scores, the P10/P50/P90 scenario tags, and the executive ranking. These are derived weightings over the captured evidence, explicitly documented by the source methodology as "analytic priors, not factual probabilities."
  • Requires Verification (flagged in the source data itself) — Qualification timing, customer ramp/yield figures, and exact HBM4E SKU specifications across all three companies — these are the fields the dataset's own evidence labels mark as unconfirmed.
  • What this report does not do — declare a market-share winner, predict specific launch dates, or treat Micron's zero HBM4E score as evidence of absence rather than evidence of a data gap.
