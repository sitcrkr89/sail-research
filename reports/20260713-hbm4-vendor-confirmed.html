HBM4 Goes Commercial: All Three Vendors Now Confirm the Generation Transition

Jul 13, 2026Sail ResearchSamsung · SK hynix · Micron, 3 primary disclosuresPublic-source evidence review

For most of 2026, “HBM4” lived in roadmap language — slides, timelines, intent. As of the disclosures we captured on July 12, that is no longer the honest description. All three memory vendors now maintain live, official, first-party pages that treat HBM4 as a present-tense product, and one of them asserts commercial shipment outright.

The Thesis

The HBM4 generation transition is now vendor-confirmed across all three manufacturers — it has left the roadmap and entered the market. This is a deliberately narrow claim. It says nothing about who wins, what volumes ship, or which customers have qualified parts. It says: the generational handoff itself is no longer speculative, because each vendor is publicly staking its own name on it.

Decision Relevance

  • Strategy teams can now treat HBM4 as an active generation transition, while keeping vendor leadership and qualification outcomes open.
  • Supply-chain teams should shift monitoring from roadmap announcements toward qualification, volume, yield, packaging, and customer-specific evidence.
  • Investors and market researchers should not infer shipment share or pricing power from product-page presence alone.

Three sources, three independent first-party publishers, three different kinds of commitment — a shipment claim, an ecosystem demonstration, a product listing. Under our evidence rules these are independent supports for the same thesis, which is what qualifies it as a release-grade card rather than a monitoring note.

The Counter-Evidence We Are Carrying Forward

Our own prior report (SR-2026-0005) cuts against over-reading this thesis, and we attach that counter-evidence to the card rather than quietly dropping it:

  • Samsung’s HBM4E 16-high / 64 GB variant remains plan-only — a delayed watch item in the earlier public scenario analysis, despite the company’s commercial-first claim.
  • SK hynix’s 16-layer path carries the highest relative risk in that public scenario analysis.
  • Micron’s HBM4E coverage remains a public-evidence gap, not evidence of absence — the cited sources do not yet establish the same level of detail.

In short: the generation is confirmed; the next generation’s hardest configurations are not.

What This Report Cannot Tell You

Shipment volumes, yields, per-customer qualification status, and pricing are all undisclosed by every vendor. Any analysis quoting numbers for those today is modeling, not reporting. We flag them as open verification gaps on the card.

Evidence Caveat — Verification Boundary

The verified thesis is limited to the cross-vendor generation transition. It does not verify shipment volume, yield, customer qualification, market share, or pricing. Those remain open evidence gaps.

Method Note — Public Release Standard

Each supporting source was captured directly from the vendor’s own site and checked for provenance, freshness, claim boundaries, and publication policy before human approval. Sail Research requires at least two independent primary sources for a cross-vendor thesis; this report uses three.

  • HBM4E 16-high and advanced-packaging disclosures that change the current risk picture.
